FORT BREACH

October 3rd, 1949. Fort Sentinel was sealed overnight — the 112th Engineer Regiment marched out at dawn and never came back. Locked in the vault of the inner keep they left their regimental colors… and your grandfather's service journal.

Tonight, seventy years later, the Army has signed the fort over to the wreckers. The demolition convoy arrives at first light. "Nothing inside is worth the paperwork," they told you. They didn't ask the family of the 112th.

You are a retired combat engineer, and you kept your kit. The fort is empty of people — but its automated security still hums along on one stubborn diesel generator: a sweeping searchlight, seismic ground sensors, magnetic locks on every door. Get in. Cut the power. Open the vault. Bring the colors home before dawn.
RULES OF THE NIGHT — harm nothing · take only what's yours · leave only footprints
MOVE: WASD / Arrow keys  ·  USE TOOL / OPEN: E or Space  ·  Walk up to an obstacle for a hint.
Stay out of the searchlight and sensor rings — three alerts and the night is blown.
